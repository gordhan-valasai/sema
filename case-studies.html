Case Studies

Selected projects demonstrating optimisation-based analysis

1. Green Hydrogen Adoption in Pakistan's Heavy-Duty Freight Sector

Method: Agent-based model Data: NTRC freight transport data Client: Research collaboration

Problem

Assess the feasibility and optimal pathway for transitioning Pakistan's heavy-duty freight vehicle fleet to green hydrogen fuel cell vehicles, considering infrastructure requirements, cost competitiveness, and adoption dynamics.

Approach

Developed an agent-based model with heterogeneous fleet operators making adoption decisions based on total cost of ownership, infrastructure availability, and peer effects. Calibrated against historical vehicle registration data from NTRC. Integrated hourly electrolyser dispatch optimisation to determine hydrogen production costs.

Key Results

  • Optimal electrolyser capacity and technology mix for freight corridor coverage
  • Adoption timeline under different policy scenarios (subsidies, carbon pricing)
  • Levelised cost of hydrogen competitive with diesel at specific electricity price thresholds
  • Infrastructure roll-out sequence prioritising high-traffic corridors

Limitations and Caveats

  • Model assumes rational cost-minimising agents; behavioural factors not fully captured
  • Infrastructure cost estimates based on international benchmarks; local conditions may differ
  • Electricity price projections subject to policy uncertainty
  • Water availability constraints for electrolysis not explicitly modelled in all scenarios

2. GCC Hydrogen Export Corridor Optimisation

Method: Multi-objective MILP Focus: Qatar as supplier Client: Research collaboration

Problem

Design optimal hydrogen export infrastructure from Qatar to European markets, balancing cost minimisation, emissions reduction, and water use constraints.

Approach

Multi-objective mixed-integer linear programming model optimising production capacity, storage, and transport mode selection (pipeline vs. shipping). Incorporated hourly solar resource variability from ERA5 data and demand uncertainty through stochastic scenarios.

Key Results

  • Optimal production capacity and technology mix (alkaline vs. PEM electrolysers)
  • Trade-offs between cost, emissions, and water consumption
  • Transport mode selection sensitive to distance and volume
  • Storage requirements to manage renewable variability

Limitations and Caveats

  • Export demand scenarios based on European policy targets; actual demand may differ
  • Pipeline cost estimates for long-distance hydrogen transport subject to technical uncertainty
  • Water desalination costs and energy requirements simplified
  • Geopolitical and regulatory risks not quantified

3. UK Hydrogen Infrastructure Under Demand Uncertainty

Method: Two-stage stochastic programming (UKH2-Stoch) Risk Measure: CVaR Client: Research collaboration

Problem

Determine optimal hydrogen production and storage infrastructure for the UK under uncertain future demand from industry, transport, and power sectors.

Approach

Two-stage stochastic MILP with CVaR risk measures. First stage: infrastructure investment decisions. Second stage: operational dispatch under demand scenarios. Generated scenarios from UK government projections and sensitivity analysis.

Key Results

  • Risk-averse infrastructure investment recommendations
  • Value of stochastic solution indicating significant benefit over deterministic planning
  • Optimal storage capacity to manage demand uncertainty
  • Sensitivity analysis on risk aversion parameters

Limitations and Caveats

  • Demand scenarios based on policy projections; actual sectoral demand may evolve differently
  • Model assumes perfect foresight in second stage; operational flexibility may be constrained
  • Grid integration constraints simplified
  • Technology cost learning not endogenously modelled

4. Climate-Constrained Power System Planning

Method: TIMES with climate-adjusted resources Focus: Water-energy interactions Client: Research collaboration

Problem

Long-term power system capacity expansion planning under carbon constraints, incorporating climate-adjusted renewable resource availability and water use constraints.

Approach

TIMES model with climate-adjusted solar and wind profiles from ERA5 and NASA POWER. Integrated water-energy nexus constraints for thermal power plants and renewable resource variability under climate change scenarios.

Key Results

  • Technology mix and capacity expansion schedule to 2050
  • Impact of climate-adjusted renewable resources on optimal capacity
  • Water use trade-offs between thermal and renewable technologies
  • Cost and emissions trajectories under different carbon budgets

Limitations and Caveats

  • Climate projections based on RCP scenarios; actual climate outcomes may differ
  • Water availability constraints simplified; detailed hydrological modelling not included
  • Transmission constraints and grid stability not explicitly modelled
  • Technology cost projections subject to uncertainty
